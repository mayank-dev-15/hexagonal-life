About Hexagonal CA
Hexagonal cellular automata use a grid of hexagons where each cell has exactly 6 neighbors (vs 4 in square grids). This creates more isotropic behavior — patterns evolve equally in all directions. Conway's classic rules adapt naturally: birth with 2 neighbors, survival with 2-3. The hex topology produces unique gliders, oscillators, and spaceships not possible on square grids. Try different rule sets to explore the rich emergent behavior!
